Our Story

  • The Proposal



    Courtney and I met while climbing in the Bay Area and ended up moving out to Boulder to have more access to the outdoors we love. In our first few years here we spent each weekend either climbing, hiking, camping or some combination of the three. However, after Courtney's fall she quit climbing with little intention of picking it back up.


    This continued for a time but after a 3 year hiatus, Courtney felt comfortable enough to try her hand at climbing outside again. For the Fourth of July weekend in 2016, we decided to go to one of our favorite places to camp, Eleven Mile Canyon. My plan was to put up a climb that had an anchor that would be hidden from the ground but would still be easy enough that Courtney could finish it. The climb that matched the criteria was Hollow-Flake on Arch Rock. For people who climb in Colorado, its right next to the popular route Staircase.


    On Saturday morning the weather wasn't great, it kept sprinkling which made us have to postpone climbing and just hang out in the van. If it wasn't for my plan we probably would have bailed on climbing but instead waited for a bit of good weather. Once the weather broke, I rushed us over to the climb and lead a precariously damp route to the top.


    Next, it was up to to Courtney to make it up to the top. I definitely had to coax her into finishing the climb. As she approached the top she saw a note attached to the anchor and initially thought it was just Dave being sweet putting a note up saying congrats on finishing her first climb in years. However, after reading the entire note and seeing the turquoise ring behind it she realized why I was so persistent about getting in a climb despite the rain. Due the thrill of making it to the top compounded with a proposal, Courtney came back down shaking in excitement - and thankfully said Yes.
